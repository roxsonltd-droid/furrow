Tomato Harvest 2026: When Iran Leaves the Table

May 2026 7 min read Furrow Markets Desk

Processing tomato output is set to fall to 39.8 million tonnes in 2026 — about 13% below the 2024 peak — while an indefinite Iranian export ban removes roughly 240,000 tonnes of paste from world trade.

At a glance (2026)

  • 39.8 M t — WPTC global processing forecast
  • −13% vs 2024 peak crop
  • €137/t — indicative Italy ex-field reference
  • ~240 K t — Iranian paste exports off the market

Production forecast by country

Italy remains the largest single processor, but China’s projected volume is nearly as large.

Iran’s export ban

Tehran’s indefinite agri-food export halt from 3 March 2026 removes a low-cost paste supplier from regional trade.

Who picks up the volume?

Four corridors capture most of the tonnage. Geographic logic points to China and Türkiye in the short run.

Main replacement corridors

  • Iraq (~80 K t) — China, Türkiye, UAE re-export
  • Afghanistan (~34 K t) — China, Pakistan re-export
  • Russia (~4.5 K t) — China, Türkiye, Uzbekistan
  • UAE hub (~6 K t) — China, Egypt

Beneficiary ranking

Ranked by ability to absorb displaced Iranian volume at scale:

Rank Country Edge
1 China Excess stocks, lowest offer
2 Türkiye Proximity, Gulf logistics
3 Egypt Year-round supply
4 Uzbekistan Central Asia routes
5 Italy / Spain EU quality, premium buyers
